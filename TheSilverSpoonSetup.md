"If it works, it works.. Right?"

A family of 4 living in a cozy 61m² apartment reached out to their friend Hembo, a well-meaning but overzealous IT enthusiast, to set up their home network. 
They trusted him completely. 
Two years later their electricity bills seem unusually high, the equipment in the hallway closet looks like it belongs in a data center, 
and the constant fan noise is driving everyone crazy.
They've come to you for a second opinion. 
Help the family design a more suitable solution to their problem.

This challenge will touch upon:
- Network equipment and their use cases
- Cost effectiveness and Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)
- Power consumption and running costs
- Right-sizing infrastructure to fit the actual requirement

Things to consider:
- What does the family actually need?
- What features are they paying for that they'll never use?
- How much could they save over 3 years with a better solution?

There is no single correct answer.
